SECTION B: EXTENDED DATASET (Two Zero-Cost Additions)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**Addition 1: Condenser Water Supply Temperature (CDWST)**

| Parameter | Symbol | Unit | Physics Requirement | Why Material | ASHRAE Ref | 
|-----------|--------|------|---------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Cond Supply Temp** | CDWST | °C | Condenser approach & tower performance | Enables heat rejection optimization analysis | 90.1 §6.4.1.2.1 |

**Why This Is Critical:**

From thermodynamics, chiller efficiency is dominated by **lift**:

    COP_theoretical ∝ 1 / (T_condense - T_evaporate)

In practical terms:

    Lift ≈ CDWRT - CHWST

But **condenser effectiveness** is determined by:

    Condenser Approach = CDWST - Ambient_wetbulb

**Energy Impact:**
- Each 1°C reduction in ECWT (entering condenser water) = 0.5-0.8% COP improvement
- CDWST data allows you to calculate: "Your cooling tower is delivering water 3-4°C above theoretical minimum"
- This quantifies **tower-side savings potential** independently from chiller

**Mechanical/Operational Impact:**
- CDWST elevation over time indicates **tower fouling, scaling, or biofilm** (maintenance need)
- Correlating CDWST with ambient wet-bulb reveals **tower bypass or circulation issues**

**Client Impression:**
"Your cooling tower approach is 4.2°C vs. design 2.8°C. That's $18,000/year in avoidable 
chiller energy from tower underperformance - cleanable tubes, biocide treatment, or fan blade fouling."

**Acquisition Effort:** 
- ✅ Already measured by every water-cooled chiller
- ✅ Same sensor type as CDWRT (thermistor/RTD)
- ✅ Standard BACnet/Modbus point
- ✅ **ZERO additional hardware cost**

**Addition 2: Guide Vane Position (Centrifugal) or VFD Speed (Magnetic/Screw)**

| Parameter | Symbol | Unit | Physics Requirement | Why Material | ASHRAE Ref |
|-----------|--------|------|---------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Guide Vane Pos** or **VFD Speed** | GVP or N | % or RPM | Compressor capacity modulation & control behavior | Enables load/control efficiency analysis | 90.1 §6.2.1.3 |

**Why This Is Critical:**

Centrifugal compressors modulate capacity via **guide vanes** (0-100%), which directly impacts:

**Part-Load Efficiency Curve:**
```
    COP vs. Loading %

    At 100% GVP:  COP = 5.2 (design point)
    At 50% GVP:   COP = 4.1 (15% loss)
    At 25% GVP:   COP = 2.8 (46% loss)
    At <15% GVP:  COP = 1.2 (77% loss - severe penalty)
```

**Energy Impact:**
- If average guide vane position is <25%, chiller is operating in **deeply inefficient zone**
- This indicates either:
  a) **Low building load** → Opportunity for consolidation/staging (savings opportunity)
  b) **Control hunting** → Oscillating between 15-30% → Energy waste + mechanical wear

- Guide vane position **directly quantifies** whether you're in the efficient region or not

**Mechanical/Operational Impact:**
- **Hunting patterns** (rapid oscillation 15-30%) indicate:
  - Chiller too large for actual load
  - Control valve hunting upstream
  - **Result**: 7-12% efficiency loss + accelerated bearing wear

- **Steady-state low position** (<15% for extended periods) indicates:
  - Flow restriction (mechanical fault) OR
  - Actual low load condition (efficiency opportunity)
  - **Can now distinguish** with other data (power, temps)

**Client Impression:**
"Your chiller hunts between 18-35% guide vane position every 4-6 minutes. This control 
instability is costing you 8% efficiency and will cause compressor bearing damage within 
12-18 months - estimated repair: $45,000."

**Acquisition Effort:**
- ✅ **Already measured** (control output signal)
- ✅ Not a sensor - it's the compressor control setpoint
- ✅ Standard BACnet/Modbus point on all modern chillers
- ✅ **ZERO additional hardware cost**
